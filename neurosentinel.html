CTE vs Alzheimer's Disease — Plasma Biomarker Differential
Clinical rationale: Both CTE and Alzheimer's disease are tauopathies, making differential diagnosis difficult on tau alone. However, plasma p-tau217 and the Aβ42/40 ratio separate the two: AD shows markedly elevated p-tau217 (often > 0.6 pg/mL) and reduced Aβ42/40 (< 0.06), while CTE typically shows only mild p-tau217 elevation with preserved Aβ42/40. The p-tau202/p-tau396 ratio further distinguishes — significantly higher in CTE due to differential phosphorylation at the Ser202 epitope.

NeuroSentinel™ Architecture

01

Plasma Sample Collection

Standard EDTA venous blood draw (4 mL). Plasma separated within 2 hours, frozen at -80°C until assay.

02

Simoa Single-Molecule Array

Quanterix HD-X platform. Femtomolar sensitivity for p-tau217, p-tau181, NfL, GFAP, UCH-L1. Custom assay for p-tau202/396 ratio.

03

NeuroSentinel ML Classifier

Multi-marker XGBoost ensemble trained on UNITE consortium data (DIAGNOSE-CTE, NEwTON, BU-CTE Center). Outputs probabilistic CTE/AD/mixed/control classification.

04

Claude AI Clinical Reasoning

Integrates exposure history, NINDS 2021 TES criteria, and biomarker signatures to produce clinical decision-support narratives for the treating neurologist.
